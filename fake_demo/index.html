Demo Patient: Dadu (Grandfather)

Age: 82 years old  |  Conditions: Type 2 Diabetes, Congestive Heart Failure, Atrial Fibrillation, Chronic Kidney Disease Stage 3, Hypertension

This demo compares three approaches to creating a caregiver-friendly summary for a complex elderly patient on 12 medications. The goal is to help you evaluate which approach better serves family caregivers and household helpers (Ayahs) managing daily care.

Concept A: One page (with omissions) | Concept B: Multi-page detailed | Concept C: Time-bucket + SOS (ultra-simple for India)

Key Question for Feedback

Which approach would you recommend to your patients' families? What concerns do you have about each?

Concept A: One-Page Fridge Sheet

Current approach: Everything on one printable page with hard limits.

Limits Applied:

  • Maximum 8 medications (4 omitted)
  • Maximum 3 lab results
  • Maximum 2 actions per time bucket

Potential Benefits:

  • Single page - easy to post on fridge
  • Less overwhelming for stressed caregivers
  • Forces prioritization

Potential Risks:

  • 4 medications silently dropped
  • AI decides what's "important"
  • Missing info could cause errors

Concept B: Multi-Page Care Kit

Alternative approach: Role-optimized pages with no omissions.

Four Pages:

  • Medications - Simple grid for Ayah (all 12)
  • Labs - Context for caregiver (all results)
  • Care Gaps - Actions by urgency (all items)
  • Connections - How meds/labs/gaps relate

Potential Benefits:

  • Nothing omitted - complete information
  • Role-appropriate (Ayah vs. Family)
  • Connections page shows relationships

Potential Risks:

  • 4 pages to manage
  • More complex to use
  • May overwhelm some caregivers
View Concept B →

Concept C: Time-Bucket + SOS

"Made for Dummies": Ultra-simple, time-oriented, emergency-ready.

Two Pages Only:

  • Medications - Grouped by time (8 AM, 2 PM, 6 PM, Bedtime)
  • SOS - Emergency actions caregiver CAN do

Potential Benefits:

  • Time-oriented = natural workflow
  • Emergency contact in RED at top
  • Pre-authorized SOS interventions
  • Minimal cognitive load

Potential Risks:

  • Less detail than Concept B
  • Family needs separate info
  • May not fit Kaggle demo

Questions for Your Feedback

  1. Completeness vs. Simplicity: For a patient on 12 medications, would you rather the caregiver have a complete list or a curated "top 8"?
  2. Role Separation: Does it make sense to have a simpler medication grid for household helpers (Ayahs) vs. more detailed pages for family caregivers?
  3. Trust & Safety: What would make you trust an AI-generated care summary enough to recommend it to patients?
